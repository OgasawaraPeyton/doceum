Meet Seko

Peyton:
“This all started for me in summer school in 2019 when I took a course at Punahou called Capstones, Capstones is a required course that involves community service and a service based project as the final. It was a class no one cared about and even then I did not care for, little did I know that the next few weeks of that course would impact my life in such a dramatical way. During the final my friend asked if I could help interview a kid at his volunteer site “Hawaii Literacy”. The interview was directed to an 11 year old boy named Seko, at first glance Seko looks like any ordinary boy, but as the interview progressed we began to see the the struggles he was fighting through. Seko lives in the Mayor Wrights Housing or as the kids there call it “Most Wanted Hood”. This housing community has an extremely high rate of domestic violence, thefts, and police presence. During the interview Seko told us of his struggles academically due to the fact that most of his free time is spent working at his mothers shop. You see Seko’s mother has chronic back pain and does not have the capital to afford medical care and medication so Seko helps his mother at work so that she does not have to strain her back doing some of the work. What really hit me the most was the reason Seko goes to the library during what little free time he has. Seko said that he wants to further his education not so that he can buy his mom medication or that he wants to live in a nice house, but so that he can help kids in other countries who have it worse than he has it. It was Seko’s story and passion that made me reevaluate my life, I asked myself what would happened if kids like Seko had access to better educational resources? What would happen if kids like Seko had an opportunity to find their passion? This is when I met Tae and Dillion who both had similar passions and with diverse skill sets, they helped me build a company that would be able to “ignite” a kids potential, by giving kids like Seko the educational tools they deserve, all while simultaneously reducing the amount of e-waste. This is the vision of We Ignite Change, giving kids the tools they need to find their passion, then being the spark that ignites kids potential to change the world, we change the world by uplifting others.”
